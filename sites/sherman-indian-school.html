Funding Fluctuations

The No Child Left Behind Act (NCLB 2004) “required schools to show adequate yearly progress in closing the achievement gap on standardized tests (between whites and natives Americans)”. If this goal isn't met result in penalties, including termination of employment. In an attempt to meet these goals within the time frame required many schools choose to focus education on reading, math and writing, removing the way that teachers were connecting the learning to the students’ lives. Like other schools, native american education attempted to meet the requirements by deprioritizing Native languages and cultures. (Bahr p118) In 2006, the Native Languages Preservation Act was signed, provided funding for new programs to help prevent loss of native languages and cultures. 2009 the Waiver plan was implemented allowing states to submit plans for reforming their education to the department of education. In 2010 a new budget was created increasing funding to Indian education by 8,9 million. Sherman Indian High School was faced cuts, forcing the school to lay off 34 employees. LEaving the school with only 20 instructors for 363 students. Although in 2013 funds were increased for indian education, none of these were allocated to go to off-reservation boarding schools. From 2006 to 2009 the number of students dropped from 640-350 students, this was a result of personal, family or tribal problems. Athletics

Aligned with the school's history, sports have continued to play a major role in student life. During the fall the school has boys and girls cross country, girls volleyball and boys football. In the Winter there are separate boys and girls basketball teams. Lastly during the spring there is boys and girls track and field, girls softball and boys basketball. Along with these sports the school also maintains the proper recreational areas for each other. Including tennis courts, an olympic size indoor swimming pool, pool tables, poker and domino tournaments.
